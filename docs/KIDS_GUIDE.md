# 🌟 Graylinx: A Simple Guide for Everyone!

Hi there! This guide explains all the big words we use at Graylinx in a way that is super easy to understand. Imagine Graylinx is like a "Super Brain" that helps keep a giant building (like your school or a big office) cool and comfortable.

---

## 🏢 The Building Parts

### 1. HVAC
*   **What it means:** Heating, Ventilation, and Air Conditioning.
*   **Easy English:** The building's "Weather System."
*   **Example:** It’s what makes the air cold in the summer and warm in the winter.

### 2. Hierarchy
*   **What it means:** Organization of locations.
*   **Easy English:** The "Family Tree" of the building.
*   **Example:** Building ➜ Floor 2 ➜ Science Room ➜ Air Conditioner.

### 3. iBMS
*   **What it means:** Integrated Building Management System.
*   **Easy English:** The "School Principal."
*   **Example:** It watches over everything—lights, air, and safety—to make sure everyone is doing their job correctly.

---

## ❄️ The Big Machines

### 4. Chiller
*   **What it means:** A machine that cools water.
*   **Easy English:** A "Giant Ice Box."
*   **Example:** It works like your refrigerator at home, but it's huge and cools the whole building.

### 5. AHU (Air Handling Unit)
*   **What it means:** A machine that moves air.
*   **Easy English:** A "Giant Fan."
*   **Example:** It takes the cold air from the Chiller and blows it through big pipes into different rooms.

### 6. VAV (Variable Air Volume)
*   **What it means:** A box that controls air for one room.
*   **Easy English:** A "Smart Window."
*   **Example:** If one room is too cold, the VAV closes its "window" a little bit so less cold air comes in.

---

## 📊 The Math and Data

### 7. Telemetry
*   **What it means:** Data sent from far away.
*   **Easy English:** "Live News" from the machines.
*   **Example:** A sensor tells the computer, "Hey! The science room is 25 degrees right now!"

### 8. KW (Kilowatts)
*   **What it means:** A measure of power.
*   **Easy English:** "Fuel Usage."
*   **Example:** Like how much gas a car uses, KW tells us how much electricity the machines are eating.

### 9. TR (Tons of Refrigeration)
*   **What it means:** A measure of cooling.
*   **Easy English:** "Cooling Power."
*   *Example:** If a machine has 100 TR, it means it can cool as much as 100 giant blocks of ice!

### 10. iKW/TR (Efficiency)
*   **What it means:** Power used per unit of cooling.
*   **Easy English:** "Gas Mileage."
*   **Example:** A good car goes far with a little gas. A good machine cools a lot with a little electricity.

---

## 🧠 How it Thinks

### 11. Setpoint
*   **What it means:** A target value.
*   **Easy English:** The "Goal."
*   **Example:** If you set the thermostat to 22 degrees, that is your Setpoint. The machine will work until it hits that goal.

### 12. Alarms
*   **What it means:** A warning signal.
*   **Easy English:** A "Red Flag."
*   **Example:** If a machine gets too hot, it waves a red flag (an alarm) to tell the grown-ups to come fix it.

### 13. CPM (Central Plant Management)
*   **What it means:** Software that controls the cooling plant.
*   **Easy English:** The "Smart Brain."
*   **Example:** It decides which Chiller should turn on or off so the building stays cool without wasting money.

### 14. BACnet
*   **What it means:** A communication protocol.
*   **Easy English:** The "Secret Language" of machines.
*   **Example:** It’s how the big fan talks to the giant ice box so they can work together.

---

## 💻 The Computer Stuff

### 15. Frontend
*   **Easy English:** The "Face."
*   **Example:** The colorful buttons and charts you see on the screen.

### 16. Backend
*   **Easy English:** The "Brain behind the face."
*   **Example:** The part that does all the hard math and remembers all the numbers.

### 17. API
*   **Easy English:** The "Telephone Line."
*   **Example:** How the "Face" asks the "Brain" for information. "Hey Brain, what's the temperature?"
